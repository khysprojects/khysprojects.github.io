Surrey Housing Analysis

This project analysis aims to explore housing trends across local authorities in Surrey using publicly available datasets. It focuses on key metrics such as rental prices, housing tenure, affordability, and homelessness to better understand housing pressure and market differences. The project examines these through data cleaning, data processing, modelling, and clear visualisation, allowing decisions to be made with confidence.

Affordability

Overall Recommendation

Implement a fully segmented, risk-based lending strategy that tailors decisions and products based on credit score, debt-to-income, loan-to-income, and applicant profile.

  • Grow safely by prioritizing strong profiles: Target applicants with high credit scores, low DTI, and stable employment (especially older segments) for higher approvals and premium products.
  • Control risk using clear thresholds and combined metrics: Use credit score + DTI + loan-to-income together to identify high-risk applicants and apply stricter approval rules where needed.
  • Maximize approvals through tailored offers and segmentation: Adjust loan terms, amounts, and products based on applicant type (e.g., students, self-employed, younger applicants) instead of using a single approval rule.

  • Overall, the goal is to balance growth and risk by making smarter, data-driven decisions tailored to each applicant segment.
