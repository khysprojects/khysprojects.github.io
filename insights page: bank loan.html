Bank Loan Insights

This project analyzes loan application data to understand the factors involved in loan approval decisions. It looks at variables such as credit score, debt-to-income ratio, loan amount, and applicant characteristics to explore how applications differ across the dataset and what lenders take into account when assessing them.

Credit Risk and Affordability

Key Insights

  • Strong negative relationship: As debt-to-income (DTI) increases, approval rate drops sharply. This is one of the clearest patterns in your dataset.
  • High approval “safe zone”: For DTI roughly between 0.05 – 0.30, approval rates stay relatively high (~60%–75%) and fairly stable.
  • Tipping point around 0.35–0.40: After this range, approval rates begin to decline rapidly, indicating a likely internal risk threshold.
  • Steep drop beyond 0.45: Once DTI exceeds 0.45, approval rates fall significantly (~20%–40%) and become more volatile.
  • Zone 0.50 is highly risky : At DTI above 0.50, approval rates drop to ~10%–25%, suggesting near-automatic rejection in many cases.
  • Low DTI variability: At very low DTI (<0.05), approval rates vary more—likely due to other factors (credit score, income, etc.) dominating decisions.
  • Non-linear relationship: The decline is not gradual—it shows a threshold effect, meaning lenders treat DTI as a cutoff-based rule, not just a continuous factor.
