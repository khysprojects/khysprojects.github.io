Retails Q1 performance

This project analyzes laptop sales performance across multiple stores to identify key revenue trends, top-selling products, and store performance differences over time. The goal of the analysis is to provide actionable insights that could help business stakeholders better understand sales patterns and improve decision-making related to inventory management, marketing strategies, and store operations. The dataset contains transactional sales data including product categories, sales revenue, units sold, dates of purchase, and store locations. Using this data, I explored overall revenue performance, identified the most profitable product categories, and compared sales performance between different stores.

Dashboard

Q1 Performance

Key Insights

  • Total revenue for Q1 reached £785.4K.
  • The steepest growth occurred between 15 and 19 March, when revenue jumped from £5.1K to £28.2K.
  • Wednesday was the best-performing day with highest revenue peak (£30.0K).
  • Despite overall growth, revenue fluctuated significantly throughout the quarter.

Recommendations

  • Plan more consistent sales and marketing activities to reduce revenue fluctuations.
  • Apply successful tactics from high-growth periods to improve performance during slower weeks.
  • Investigate why Thursdays and Fridays underperform and test targeted offers to boost sales on these days.

  • Top performing Category

    Key Insights

    • Laptops generated the highest revenue (£330.7K), making up 42.1% of total sales.
    • Accessories recorded the lowest revenue (£8.7K) and made up only a very small share of total sales.
    • Overall, most revenue comes from the main product categories, with laptops clearly leading performance.

    Recommendations

    • Support growth in phones and tablets through targeted promotions or bundled offers
    • Review the accessories strategy, as this category currently contributes very little revenue.
    • Reduce reliance on laptops by strengthening other product categories for long-term stability.

    • Product Performance

      Key Insights

      • Galaxy Tab was the best-selling product with 149 units sold, accounting for 35.9% of total units sold.
      • Samsung S23 recorded 130 units sold, around 14.6% fewer units than Galaxy Tab.
      • Sales among the top three products are closely distributed, indicating balanced customer demand (£30.0K).

      Recommendations

      • Ensure strong stock availability for Galaxy Tab, as it leads unit sales and drives a large share of demand.
      • Use chargers as add-on or bundled products to increase overall sales value.
      • Monitor performance of top products closely, as small changes could quickly affect rankings.

      • Store Unit Selling

        Key Insights

        • Store B sold the highest number of units (445), accounting for 35.7% of total units sold.
        • Store A recorded the lowest unit sales (392), around 13.5% fewer units than Store B.
        • Overall, unit sales are fairly balanced across stores, with moderate differences in performance.

        Recommendations

        • Leverage Store B’s strong performance by identifying best practices that can be applied across other stores.
        • Support Store C with targeted actions to help close the gap with the top-performing store.
        • Review opportunities to improve Store A’s sales, such as local promotions or stock optimisation.
